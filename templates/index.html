Skills aquired in my Quality Assurance Role

  • Helped develop the automation framework for MadCap Flare (software from previous employer)
    • The framework was setup in a way that allows any member of the QA team to put together an automation flow without really having to know automation.
    • The above was accomplished by automating as many of the usual steps/procedures that the software handles. For instance, there are flows available to open the application, load a specific project, build a specific output etc.
    • Automating a new bug is as simple as stringing the flows that cover each step in reproducing the bug!
    • This approach allowed for bugs and tests to be automated quickly and with little to no error since each step is thoroughly tested. No need to reinvent the wheel every time!
    • Last but not least, I also provided Templates for all major kinds of testing. My goal is always to make automating as accessible for team members as possible.
  • Created comprehensive test plans for all new and existing features
    • I have experience writing detailed test plans that ensures that every moving part in the new/existing feature is tested.
    • I have written these plans from the ground up, but I have also updated older test plans to help testing be more robust.
    • I have made it a habit to update test plans after the feature goes out as well. There is always room for improvements and it ensures re-testing of the new feature is as smooth as possible.
    • If possible, I also like attending planning meetings so I can get an idea of how the new feature will operate.
  • Manual testing
    • Manual testing is always the first step and I have a lot of experience in this area.
    • I follow the design specifications in order to ensure the feature is working as intended./li>
    • At this stage, I also like to check compatibility with other modules and workflows. The new feature must play well with all other components.
  • Bug documentation
    • A good bug has to provide the developer with a clear picture of the issue, its causes, as well as the desired behavior in the end.
    • I proud myself in writing detailed, concise, yet easy to read bugs.
    • I am also a firm believer in providing screenshots or screen recordings to better demonstrate a behavior that may be difficult to reproduce.
  • Bug verification
    • After a bug is fixed, my responsibility is to ensure that the fix is valid and that no new issues are introduced by it.
    • I made it a habit to read the commits so I could see what has changed in the code. This helped me narrow down the impact/scope of the fix.
  • Worked closely with other teams in the company such as Tech Support, Management, and Developers

    • I would then assess these bugs and get in touch with management and the development team if needed.
